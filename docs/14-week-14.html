14.3 Cherry Picking Issues

Gish Gallop: A debating technique that attempts to overwhelm the listener with as many arguments as possible without any regard for accuracy or coherency.

Alberto Brandolini’s Law of bullshit asymmetry: The amount of energy necessary to refute bullshit is an order of magnitude bigger than to produce it.

Cherry Picking: Selectively looking at data that supports your position while ignoring evidence that is counter to it.

For an example of all of these, check out this opinion article published by the Washington Examiner. Notice you can click on the highlights to see the commentary by climate scientists that took the time to discuss what the best scientific consensus is on each point. It is exhausting.

  1. Climate change can’t be true because:
    1. In 2019, Ted Cruz claims there hasn’t been any global warming since 1998
    2. This claim is based on Satellite data from 1997-2014. Those years are chosen very carefully. He further considers only Satellite data, which is widely considered less accurate than ground station data.
    3. The Satellite data has been corrected to line up with the ground measurements and the “pause” he refers to disappears even with the highly optimized start/stop points of his pause interval.
  2. Climate change can’t be true because:
    1. Regions in Georgia have cooled since the early parts of the 1900s!
    2. Yes but the rest of the country has warmed.
    3. Also, 1934 was the 2nd hottest year in the US, so comparing present day to the hottest part of the country in that particularly hot year results in seeing no increase.
